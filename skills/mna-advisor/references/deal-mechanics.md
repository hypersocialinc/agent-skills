# Deal mechanics — the paper you actually sign

The headline price is not the deal. The terms below decide how much of it you keep, how long you're exposed, and whether you can still walk. Founders routinely win the number and lose 10–20% (and months) to terms they didn't negotiate. Counsel drafts these; you must understand them.

## The LOI / term sheet is the leverage moment

Most economic and structural terms get *anchored* in the non-binding LOI / term sheet — re-trading them later is expensive and signals weakness. Negotiate hard here, while you still have alternatives.

- **Lock in the LOI:** price *and* structure, the consideration mix (cash vs. stock vs. earn-out vs. retention), escrow/holdback size and duration, indemnity caps, your role/comp, and the employee/retention pool. "We'll figure it out in the definitive agreement" means "we'll figure it out after your leverage is gone."

## Exclusivity / no-shop — your leverage peaks just before you sign it

A no-shop (typically **30–60 days**) bars you from talking to or accepting other buyers. The moment you sign, competitive tension — your single biggest lever — evaporates and the buyer knows it.

- **Do not grant exclusivity until the economic + key terms are locked** in the LOI.
- Keep the exclusivity **window short**, require good-faith progress, and avoid auto-renewal.
- Expect the buyer to push for it early; that's exactly when you should push back hardest.

## Asset vs. stock deal

- **Asset purchase** — buyer takes selected assets (often the IP) and leaves liabilities behind. Common in acqui-hires. Can be worse for seller taxes (corporate-level gain) and needs careful IP-transfer + consent handling.
- **Stock/merger** — buyer takes the whole entity. Cleaner for the seller's equity holders; possible QSBS treatment; buyer inherits liabilities (so heavier diligence + indemnity).
- The choice drives **tax, liability, and which consents you need** — settle it early; it changes everyone's net.

## Escrow / holdback

- Typically **~10% of price held 12–18 months** as security for breaches of reps/warranties.
- It's real money you don't get at close — **count it out of "proceeds"** when sizing the deal.
- Negotiate the percentage, the period, and whether it's the buyer's **sole remedy**.

## Reps & warranties, indemnification

- You warrant the company's state (IP ownership, no undisclosed liabilities, etc.). Breaches let the buyer claw back.
- Negotiate: **survival period** (12–36 months), **caps** (ideally ≤ escrow), **baskets/deductibles** (de minimis thresholds), and **knowledge/materiality qualifiers**.
- **R&W insurance (RWI)** can shift this risk to an insurer and shrink the escrow — raise it when the holdback is large.
- For an underwater deal where investors take the consideration, watch **who bears indemnity** — push for it not to fall disproportionately on the founder.

## Earn-out — discount it, then protect it

An earn-out bridges a valuation gap, but it is the **most over-valued line in any offer**. Post-close you usually don't control the levers that hit the targets (budget, roadmap, headcount, accounting), targets drift vague, and disputes have weak remedies.

- **Discount contingent/employment-tied consideration heavily** against cash. A lower all-cash offer often beats a higher one riding on two years of employment + a vague formula.
- If you take one, negotiate: **objective, measurable targets**; **control over the relevant levers** (or covenants that the buyer will operate to support them); a **dispute-resolution mechanism**; and **acceleration on termination-without-cause or breach**.
- Keep the earn-out period short and the milestones genuinely reachable.

## Quick checklist

- [ ] Economics + key terms locked in the LOI *before* exclusivity?
- [ ] No-shop window short, progress-gated, no auto-renew?
- [ ] Asset vs. stock decided — tax + liability + consents understood?
- [ ] Escrow/holdback %, duration, sole-remedy negotiated and netted out of proceeds?
- [ ] Reps survival/caps/baskets negotiated; RWI considered if holdback is big?
- [ ] Indemnity burden not dumped on the founder?
- [ ] Earn-out discounted, targets objective, levers controlled, disputes covered, accel on termination?
